27 0E 01 22 0D 00 14 0C 00 10 0C 01 11 18 01 FF <Warps of the domain. 
27 0E 01 < 27 = XX Cord, 0E = YY cord, 01 = Warp Type
-Warp types
00= Where you spawn upon entering this floor layout
01= Next Floor
02= Exit Domain
FF represents end of list
Note you can have both next floor and exit domain warps in the same layout if you wish to extend to dungeon
-Chests Types
10 0F 00 10 24 10 00 10 14 19 00 10 FF
10 0F 00 10 < 10 = XX Cord, 0F = YY Cord, 00 and 10 represent what's inside the chest as well the spawn rate
-Trap types
This domain doesn't have them on this layout for me to reference but here is how they would appear
10 20 12 22 32 42 00 00
10 20 12 22 32 42 00 00 < 10 = XX Cord, 20 = YY Cord, 12 = 25% chance for trap to spawn if not 00, 22 = 25% chance for trap to spawn if not 00, 32 = 25% chance for trap to spawn if not 00, 42 = 25% chance for trap to spawn if not 00.
12 22 32 42 represent the trap type in this example this means we have :
12 = 25% chance for a Yellow Spore
22 = 25% chance for a Green Spore
32 = 25% chance for a Blue Spore
42 = 25% chance for a Purple Spore
If we change this to 10 20 12 22 00 42 00 00 for example we now have :
12 = 25% chance for a Yellow Spore
22 = 25% chance for a Green Spore
00 = 25% chance for no trap to occupy the tile
42 = 25% chance for a Purple Spore

-Digimon Types
11 0D 10 32 26 0D 10 32 13 17 10 32 FF
11 0D 10 32 < 11 = xx Cord, 0D = yy Cord, 10 = Encounter type 1 and chance, 32 = Encounter type 2 and chance
For encounter types 10~13 is encounter group 1, 20~23 is encounter group 2, 30~33 is encounter group 3, 40~44 is encounter group 4
To cause a digimon to always spawn match it's encounter group id for example if we want the first encounter to always spawn we take 
11 0D 10 32 and make it 11 0D 11 11 but if we want encounter 1 and 4 to evenly spawn we use 11 0D 11 44

Note these will always come in these order unless there is 0, or 1 of an object in this case it will push the data to the end of the file.
